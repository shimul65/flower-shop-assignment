Types of Pink Flower Tree

Trees with pink flowers can help add some additional balance to
a well thought out gardening space.

Kanzan Cherry Tree

Chances are when you think of “cherry tree,” this is the variety you envision.
This popular variety has a vase-shaped form and a profusion of medium-to-dark
pink flowers that bloom every spring. The flowers are roughly two and a half inches
in diameter and can be seen in April and May in Zones 5 through 9.

Prairifire Flowering Crabapple

There are several different kinds of flowering crabapple, but one of our favorites is the prairifire flowering crabapple.
This stunning tree produces dark pink and red flowers, which bloom from April to May.
They also have showy foliage that ranges from a dark maroon color in
the summertime to a fiery bronze in the fall.

Weeping Cherry Tree

There is not a single pink flowering tree in this guide that is as graceful or romantic
as the weeping cherry tree. Not to be confused with a fruiting cherry tree or cherry blossom,
this showy tree is small and slender, with long branches that trail towards the ground. Every spring,
the tree bursts with pink and white single or double flowers from the black branches, usually before the leaves show up.
